What do the new tiered service plans mean for you?​

  • If you already store data on Synapse, nothing will change to your existing Synapse Project(s).
  • Nothing will change for contributions to NIH-designated repositories. For example:
    • If you want to add a new project to an existing Data Coordinating Center (e.g. AD Knowledge Portal or Project GENIE) which is already covered by a grant funding mechanism.
    • If you request to share data directly via a Sage-managed Knowledge Portal.
  • If you only download data and don't use Synapse for storing data, then you will not be impacted by any changes.
  • As of November 15, 2024, if you add data to, or require additional services for your existing Synapse Project, or create a new Synapse Project, the following changes will apply:
    • Basic Hosting Plan: If you are looking to store less than 100GB of data, nothing will change. This storage is part of our Basic Hosting Plan and is FREE.
    • Self-Managed Plan: If you are looking to store between 100GB and 500GB of data, you can sign up for a paid Self-Managed Plan. Plans include support for set-up and Governance consulting. Visit the Sage Offering Help Page for more information.
    • Data Coordination Plan: If you'd like to store over 500GB or need additional services or customized solutions, you will be part of our Data Coordination Plan, for which a customized project and quote will be generated. This includes multi-center consortiums seeking a Data Coordinating Center.

For more detailed information on the Synapse Offerings, or if you have questions about your existing project, you can visit the Sage Offering Help Page or contact us through our Service Desk.

The full Terms of Service can be reviewed here. The key changes include the following:
  1. Privacy and Data Protection: We have updated our terms to reflect current data protection regulations and clarify restrictions on posting certain types of protected health information.

  5. Prohibited Conduct: We expand the list of prohibited activities to better protect our users and services.
  6. Account Management: We establish that accounts inactive for 24 months may be suspended, and we outline a subsequent with outlined reactivation process.
  7. Training Requirements: We add requirements for the completion of additional training for some advanced features. Some advanced features may require completion of additional training.
  8. Payment Terms: We provide comprehensive details on pricing, payment procedures, and handling of delinquent accounts.
  9. Dispute Resolution: We introduce an arbitration agreement for dispute resolution, with an opt-out option.
  10. Termination Effects: We provide more details on what happens when an account is terminated.

The full Privacy Policy can be reviewed here. In summary, the key changes include the following:

Why are these changes taking effect?​

  • The new plan offerings allow us to provide users with project set-up and governance support, as well as guidance on adhering to the NIH Data Management and Sharing Policy. All of this will help support new and existing users to share, manage and reuse data.
  • As a nonprofit organization, our primary mission is to provide scientific communities with access to high-quality biomedical data to help advance human health. However, maintaining accessible data long-term does come at a cost, e.g. storage and governance services. The changes to our Synapse offerings will help offset a fraction of these costs and maintain the openness of these data for as many people as possible.
